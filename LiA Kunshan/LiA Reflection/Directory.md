# ✦ Laidlaw Leadership in Action Workspace
*Reflective Journal*

> **The LIA Perspective**
> The Leadership in Action (LIA) asks you to step beyond theory and into reial-world complexity, collaboration, and responsibility. The quality of your LIA will not only be defined by what you deliver, but by how you lead, reflect, and grow.

---

### Ⅰ . The Experience Arc

> **01 Preparation (Before You Begin)**
> Clarify your purpose, refine your SMART goals, align with relevant SDGs, and reflect on ethical engagement. Think critically about power, collaboration, and your role as a Scholar.

> **02 Weekly Reflections (During The LIA)**
> Document key moments, ethical dilemmas, leadership challenges, and lessons learned. Move beyond “what happened?” and into “what did I learn?” and “how did I lead?”

> **03 Integration (After The LIA)**
> Consolidate learning and evaluate impact. Reflect on SMART goals, systemic change, character development, and global mindset to prepare for final outputs.

---

### Ⅱ . Core Principles

* **Honesty over Performance** ── Use this space honestly; it is built for personal growth, not external evaluation.
* **Depth over Volume** ── Nuance and deep reflection matter far more than filling every single prompt perfectly.
* **Embrace Discomfort** ── Lean into the questions that feel uncomfortable; that is precisely where the growth lies.
* **Track Evolution** ── Revisit earlier entries regularly to observe how your perspective shifts over time.
