# ✦ Ethical Engagement Self-Assessment
*Just, Reciprocal, and Mindful Practice*

> As outlined in the [[Ethical Engagement Guidelines]], ethical engagement means collaborating in ways that are just, reciprocal, deeply mindful of power dynamics, and oriented toward long-term systemic value rather than short-term aid.

![[LIA_Ethical_Engagement_2025.pdf]]

---

### Ⅰ . Community-Led & Locally-Driven
* How were the needs and focus of this project identified, and by whom?
* How does this project amplify existing local infrastructure rather than creating parallel, redundant work?
* Where might you be unintentionally centring your own ideas or academic background over local expertise?

> [!quote] Reflection
> 

---

### Ⅱ . Sustainable Impact & Continuity
* What tangible, lasting value will this project leave behind once your six weeks end?
* Are you addressing a structural barrier or merely treating an immediate symptom?
* Could your temporary presence accidentally create organizational dependency?

> [!quote] Reflection
> 

---

### Ⅲ . Skill-Based Contribution & Humility
* Do you possess the appropriate training and preparation for this specific role?
* Where do you need to actively practice intellectual humility and seek extra guidance?
* Are you stepping into operational spaces where local professionals would be better positioned?

> [!quote] Reflection
> 

---

### Ⅳ . Reciprocity & Mutual Exchange
* How is this relationship genuinely a two-way street? What are you giving, and what are you extracting?
* Are you documenting or leveraging this experience in a way that risks exploiting the community for personal academic gain?

> [!quote] Reflection
> 

---

### Ⅴ . Cultural Humility & Structural Awareness
* How does your personal identity and background shape how you are perceived in this specific context?
* What historical, socioeconomic, or political factors directly influence this community?
* What latent assumptions or systemic biases might you be bringing to the field?

> [!quote] Reflection
> 

---

### Ⅵ . Environmental & Economic Sustainability
* How are you actively minimizing your project's physical and environmental footprint?
* How are project funds being managed transparently and responsibly?
* Are your interventions realistic if external funding were to cease?

> [!quote] Reflection
> 

---

### Ⅶ . Accountability & Continuous Adaptation
* At its root, why are you undertaking this project, and who truly benefits most from it?
* How are you intentionally creating psychological safety for stakeholders to give you critical feedback?
* How will you respond if your foundational project assumptions are proven wrong?

> [!quote] Reflection
