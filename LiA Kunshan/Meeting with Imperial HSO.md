_"Over six weeks, my co-investigator and I will audit 3-5 township health centres in Kunshan, using China's national standard GBZ 130-2020 as our benchmark. We're conducting a KAP survey—Knowledge, Attitude, Practice—among radiology staff, doing structured walkthroughs of facility shielding, and then co-developing what we call a 'Zero-Cost Behavioural Safety Toolkit'—visual checklists, anti-loss dosimeter protocols, and simple reminders that work in low-resource, high-stress environments. The goal is to reduce that 52.5% behavioural error rate with interventions that cost nothing but are designed with the end-user in mind."_

**Meeting Minutes (Refined)**

**1. Giving Feedback to Staff (Non-confrontational approach)**

- Feedback should be framed as a **collaborative problem-solving process**, not instruction-giving.
- Present findings first, then invite staff to reflect on possible solutions:

- “Here is what we observed—what do you think could help address this?”

- Emphasise **shared ownership of solutions** rather than external imposition.
- Follow-up is important:

- Provide ongoing support and engagement rather than one-off corrective instructions.

---

**2. Maximising Effectiveness of the Toolkit**

- The toolkit should focus on **empowering frontline staff** to enable sustainable behavioural change.
- Aim to strengthen the **safety culture**, not just compliance.
- Encourage engagement from **middle and senior management**, not only frontline workers.
- Reinforce that **training and standard operating procedures (SOPs)** remain foundational elements, but must be practical and usable in real workflows.

---

**3. Measuring Impact / Defining Success**

- Use **pre- and post-intervention surveys** to evaluate changes in:

- Knowledge
- Attitudes
- Self-reported practices

- Consider comparing results across time points to assess behavioural change.
- Success should be defined not only by compliance improvement, but also by **changes in safety awareness and culture**.

---

**4. Understanding Safety Culture**

- Cultural assessment questions should be informed by **existing literature and validated frameworks**.
- Literature review is important to ensure questions can distinguish:

- Knowledge gaps
- Behavioural norms
- Organisational culture

---

**5. Accelerating Implementation / Fieldwork**

- Recognise and account for **differences in individuals, roles, and hospital environments**.
- Adapt communication and engagement strategies depending on context and staff hierarchy.

---

**6. Incident / Accident Investigation Approach**

- Further explore how incidents are currently investigated in practice.
- Contingency plans
- Focus on understanding:

- Root cause analysis methods
- Whether system-level factors are considered
- How findings are translated into preventive actions
